Space Definitions

W, W1, W2, W3, C, K
The first 4 infinite cardinals, the continuum (or the Cantor space or 2^W) and an arbitrary uncountable cardinal.
M, where M is an ordinal or cardinal
M is given the ordinal topology by default.
DX
The set X with the discrete topology.
(X)'
The subspace of X of all non-isolated points.
X x Y
The product of X and Y.
X^K
The K power of X.
HX
The Hyperspace of all non-empty closed subsets of X.
Sup X
The Superextension of X.
EX
The Absolute of X.
BX
The Stone-Cech cptn of X.
X*
The Stone-Cech remainder BX-X.
Lk X
The space of all complete subgraphs of the intersection graph of the clopen sets of X, with the Tychonov topology.
M. Bell, C.M.U.C. 23,3 (1982) 525-536.
Cen X
The space of all centered subcollections of the clopen sets of X, with the Tychonov topology.
M. Bell, Fund. Math. CXXV (1985) 47-58.
AX
1 pt cptn of X.
2 Lx O
2^O with the lexicographic order topology where O is an ordinal.
Path T
The space of all paths thru the tree T, with the Tychonov topology.
S. Todorcevic, Studia Math. 116,1 (1995) 49-57.
Tree T
The 1 pt cptn of the tree T with the tree topology.
2^K
2^K | 2
Collapse 2 distinct points of 2^K to a point.
2^W3 | W3+1
Collapse the closed subset of 2^W3 which consists of all functions f which are initially 0's followed by all 1's to a point.
M. Bell, C.M.U.C. 31,4 (1990) 775-779.
Pas Hom K
Pasenkov's homogeneous dyadic of weight 2^K which is not a Cantor cube.
V. Pasenkov, Soviet Math. Dokl. 15,1 (1974) 43-47.
VDou Rig K
Van Douwen's rigid dyadic space of weight K.
E. van Douwen, Top. and its Applns 47 (1992) 203-208.
Dyad Sum Rig
A rigid 1 pt cptn of a sum of countably many quite different rigid dyadic spaces.
A(K)
Two 1's K
The subspace of 2^K of all functions with at most 2 1's.
Fin 1's K
1 pt cptn of the sum of spaces X_n where n < W and X_n is the subspace of 2^K of all functions with at most n 1's.
M. Bell, C.M.U.C. 26,2 (1985) 353-361.

Aron Line

DeG Rig
De Groot and Maurice's rigid HL and HS Cor.
J. de Groot and M. Maurice, P.A.M.S. 19,4 (1968) 844-846.
Path W1
Use the tree of all functions with domain a countable successor ordinal and range W1, ordered by extension. This tree is NOT a normal tree.
Path Aron
Use an Aronszajn subtree of the tree of all increasing, well-ordered sequences of rationals with a maximal element, ordered by extension.
Path WO(Q)
Use the tree of all increasing, bounded, well-ordered sequences of rationals, ordered by extension.
Path Small K
Use a tree of small height W and having that distinct nodes have different cardinalities of successors. The parameter K is the supremum of these cardinalities.
Path Big K
Use a tree of big height W1 and having that distinct nodes have different cardinalities of successors. The parameter K is the supremum of these cardinalities.
Path Tod
Use Todorcevic's tree T of all closed subsets of W1 contained in a bistationary subset S of W1, modified by inserting countable W-ary trees between a node and its successors in T, ordered by extension.
S. Todorcevic, Handbook of Set-Theoretic Topology (1984).
Tree C
Use the binary tree of height W+1.
Tree WO(Q)
Use the tree of all increasing, bounded, well-ordered sequences of rationals, ordered by extension.
Tree W1
Use the binary tree of height W1.
